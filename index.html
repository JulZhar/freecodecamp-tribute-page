The TARDIS

Time And Relative Dimension In Space

The TARDIS is a fictional hybrid of the time machine and spacecraft that appears in the British science fiction television series Doctor Who. Its exterior appearance mimics a police box, an obsolete type of telephone kiosk that was once commonly seen on streets in Britain.

Interesting facts about TARDIS

  • The TARDIS is a living organism that grows on the planet Gallifrey.
  • The model of the TARDIS used by the Doctor lost its ability to transform due to a breakdown and remained in the form of a 1963 model police telephone booth.
  • The TARDIS receives its energy from several sources, including the core of an artificial black hole.
  • In order to fully function, the TARDIS needs to be connected to the Doctor's brain.
  • The TARDIS has the ability to understand and use all existing languages. This ability is transferred to its passengers.
  • In 1984, an asteroid was named after the TARDIS.
  • The TARDIS has the property of mimicry.
  • New Zealand in 2013, in honor of the 50th anniversary of the series, issued a silver coin with the image of the TARDIS with a face value of 2 dollars.
  • During takeoff and landing, the TARDIS makes a distinctive sound, described in the series as "the best sound in the universe."
  • Illegal use of the TARDIS is punishable by death.
